Steps to get your website done!

Step 1:
Consultation / Goal Setting

The first step is to schedule a meeting via face-to-face, phone or Skype. Think of this as the getting to know you phase. This allows me to get to know your goals and your business.

Step 2:
Site Architecture / Sitemap

Based on our first meeting, I will submit a proposal that outlines my recommendations for the pages of your website. This will include details regarding the hierarchical structure and navigational ordering of the website's pages.

Step 3:
Wireframing

Once the proposal for the sitemap is approved, I will check to see if there are any design or functionality requests that you would like to have incorporated into the site. After this, i get started on a few composite sketches of the site that outlines the basic look and feel of the website. I will submit for approval before proceeding.

Step 4:
Initial Design Mock-ups and Revisions

This is when I get creative as a freelance web designer. A composite sketch is transferred from pen & paper form to Adobe Photoshop. The design mockup is presented to the client in Photoshop format.

I provide a link for the client to view the mockup and to provide feedback to me. In some circumstances, there may be more than one version of the design and logos. I depend on our client's input at this stage, as it has been called for moving forward for completion

Step 5:
Coding the Design

Once the Photoshop mockup is approved, I then code it into a fully functional website using programming languages such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Bootstrap, and AngularJS. It will be a responsive website, which is mobile device, smartphone, tablet friendly. This means that the website is readily view of all multiple screen sizes.

Step 6:
Approval and Launch

Upon completion and approval of the new website, it will be launched onto its domain. Once live, I will submit a site map to Google.

With websites getting more complex, I always make sure to incorporate AngularJS into my websites. Why Angular? In a simplified version, AngularJS makes the website faster by implementating its single page application methods. Single-page applications are web applications that load a single HTML page and dynamically update that page as the user interacts with the app. SPAs use AJAX and HTML5 to create fluid and responsive web apps, without constant page reloads making the website much faster.
